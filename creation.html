WHO IS MY CHARACTER?
Quite simply, you can be anyone you want to be! The Milky Way is a huge place full of limitless opportunity and plenty of strange phenomena, things that may or may not be fully understood. Your character can be anyone and anything you can think of that wouldn't make the genre lean on fantasy (so no wizards!).
Alien hybrids, androids trying to learn what it means to have emotion, a marine xenobotanist who just wants to earn prestige and the respect of their peers, a bounty hunter chasing down a being that warps the laws of physics — get creative with it! You're free to invent as much about the setting as you like, including places, history, wars, species, flora & fauna, anything.

The only mandatory hook is that you work on one of two ships, controlled by two separate factions: The CTV Houdini's Ghost, a long range recon ship for the Coalition, or the RSV Fireside Chat, a Rebellion science ship carrying valuable data. It's not required that your character feel strongly either way about the war or their cause; they could've been in it for the pay, the opportunity to be on a starship, the possibility of research grants or fame, whatever else you can come up with. Whatever the reason, they're there!

THE VISUAL APPLICATION
Here's where you can show us exactly how your character looks! Art on the application should be at least waist-up. It doesn't have to be anything particularly cleaned or extravagant; so long as it gives a clear enough picture of how your character looks, it's fine.

When it comes to clothing, humanoids from the Rebellion will have uniforms corresponding to their departments, while humanoids from the Coalition will probably have whatever they could get their hands on. Non-humanoid body plans will probably have niche, specialized wear dependant on their race and job, which you're free to design yourself! For a more in-depth look at what people will probably be wearing, see our page on clothing.

The completed visual application should be submitted to DeviantArt and then linked in the text section of a join request to the group.

SKILLS
For more on how our skills work and a list of skills available, see our mechanics page.

Remember, you may only have up to tier 2 in any skill, with exceptions for high ranking officers (jobs with a star next to their title on the manifest or in the box below), who can have up to tier 3 in one skill only. You get four free tiers to allocate, in addition to those provided by your character's department and focus.

You get one free tier in one of two skills, depending on what department you're in. The relevant chart is below:

  • Helm gets 1 free tier in Lead or Pilot.
  • Engineering gets 1 free tier in Fix or Work.
  • Science gets 1 free tier in Know or Program.
  • Medical gets 1 free tier in Know or Heal.
  • Security gets 1 free tier in Shoot or Stab.

When listing your skills, put an asterisk next to the skill you selected from your department, and two asterisks next to any skill from your focus. Remember to put what tier they all are!

JOBS
HELM

Those lucky enough to be at the helm are the ones physically controlling the ship. Most helm work takes place on the bridge, which is where most of the ship's functions can be toggled, where incoming communications are received, where ship alerts are issued, and where most administrative decisions are made. The helm is the only department made up exclusively of commissioned officers.

(O-6) captain: The captain oversees operations throughout the entire ship, and as the highest ranking officer they have the final say on every matter. Typically, captains are the ones issuing commands, answering communications, and making the tough decisions. The prosperity and safety of the crew falls squarely on their shoulders.

Their job is to be an advisor and secondary figure of authority that the captain can count on to manage smaller matters. In the event of the captain's absence, the first mate is the one to take up their mantle.

SECURITY

In no other department are tensions between Coalition and Rebellion members as severe as they are in security. Where the Rebellion engineer might've simply enjoyed the stream of new projects and the Coalition scientist might've just been in it for research grants, all crew in security were soldiers who pledged their lives and loyalty to their cause: protecting their faction's interests and eliminating the enemy. Though many security members would love nothing more than to shoot and punch each other like they would otherwise, unusual circumstances have forced them to set aside their differences and work together for a change.

Security is the largest department on the ship by far, owing largely to the fact that it's two ship's military branches haphazardly smashed together. While each force's job was once simply to repel boarding crews and engage the enemy should the need arise, they now offer general tactical service and compose the main body of away teams.

(O-4) head of security: The head of security has the tall order of keeping their motley crew of soldiers organized and disciplined. In addition to the duties of a regular soldier, their job is to deal with reports of crime and misconduct, ensure their juniors keep in shape, and coordinate security efforts during a crisis.

ENGINEERING

As an engineer, every safe voyage is your personal victory. The bigwigs up at the helm might be the ones barking orders, but you're the workers putting in the elbow grease to keep this metal tiger purring. Engineering is the lifeblood of the ship, ensuring all the various systems are operational and that there aren't any damaged sections of hull. Any technical problem or equipment failure falls to the engineers to fix. Their most important task is making sure the ship's engines are outputting power at a steady rate.

(O-4) head of engineering: The head of engineering is the leader of the engineering department. In addition to the duties of a regular engineer, their job is to oversee and approve of any projects undertaken by their staff, as well as coordinate engineering efforts during a crisis.

0/1

(E-1 through E-4) engineer: When there's a hull breach, someone needs to patch it. When a power conduit gets blown out, someone has to fix it. The people with the skill and ingenuity to do so are engineers, good at thinking on their feet and working with their hands. While most people struggle to merely diagnose the various issues on a starship, let alone tinker with its guts to make it work again, engineers know the ins and outs of their ship like the back of their hand, and they aren't afraid to handle wires and gears and whatever else they need to in order to get the job done.

MEDICAL

Accidents can happen on just about any mission. Ranging from work-related injuries to straight up getting your leg chopped off by the slathering mandibles of a giant insectoid, starship crew are no strangers to peril. No matter what type of operation you're running, doctors who are brave or stupid enough to sail among the stars are indispensable - when it comes to space adventures, it's never bad to have someone around who can analyze alien physiology and treat exotic types of wounds.

(O-4) head of medicine: The head of medicine runs the medical department. In addition to the duties of a regular doctor, their job is to keep medical records up to date, handle triage during crises, assist with troubling or confusing presentations in patients, and keep tabs on the health of the crew, clueing in their doctors to any budding problems.

0/1

(E-1 through E-4) doctor: After getting shot, the person hovering over your body is probably going to be a doctor, with a diagnostic tool in one hand and life-saving medicine in the other. Though the scope of medicine is broad, all doctors have extensive knowledge of anatomy and physiology, as well as emergency care and how to perform several basic surgeries. The ship relies on its doctors to patch up wounds, keep any diseases in check, and keep the crew clinging on to life.

SCIENCE

Weird, unexpected things happen in space all the time - engineers may have a keen understanding of science in its practical application, but most engineers aren't going to understand the ins and outs of something like quantum physics or general relativity like a scientist would. Scientists are the people offering counsel on any anomalies the ship may encounter, as well as conducting pertinent research and experimenting with new technologies. Being stranded in a completely unknown area of space, scientific knowledge is about to come in handy.

(O-4) head of research: The head of research is the one making sure nobody blows up the laboratory or forgets to wear their sterilized goggles. In addition to the duties of a regular scientist, their job is to provide their researchers with direction, offer hypotheses for any anomalies, maintain secure storage of any hazardous specimens, and enforce lab safety regulations.

0/1

(E-1 through E-4) scientist: You might be a xenobotany specialist, or an expert in geothermics - regardless of the reasonable applicability of your field, if it's science you do, it's this job you take. Scientists all have some degree of understanding of basic concepts like physics and chemistry, but beyond that, the sea of fields one can enter is vast and varied, and all are useful in some way on a spacefaring research team. As a scientist, you'll be expected to figure out things that nobody understands, be it through experimentation or educated guessing - the specifics of what you do is at the whim of your superior.

RANKS
Two crewmen might have the same job, but that doesn't mean they hold the same sway on the ship. Someone with considerable experience in their field is likely to have a promotion or two up on a newcomer - this is reflected in our ranking system, based off of military pay grades, albeit simplified and changed around a bit.

There are two separate categories of pay grades: enlisted (E-) and officer (O-). Crew who are enlisted likely volunteered for service, or in the case of the Rebellion, may have been drafted. In contrast, officers are commissioned, meaning they were specifically sought out and offered their rank by their military. Thus, the lowest officer rank (O-1) is higher than the highest enlisted rank (E-4).

Your character can be any rank from E-1 to O-6, granted the slot is open. Grades O-4 and above represent positions in charge of departments, followed by the mantle of the first mate and the captain's chair respectively. O-1 to O-3 are pilots, bridge officers, or high-up members of security. Because having an officer pay grade represents having gotten the attention of the top brass, slots for officers are limited, whereas enlisted ranks are always open.

Starting out, most crew will be E-1 or E-2, but your character will encounter several opportunities for promotion along the course of the campaign.

When the RSV Fireside Chat crew assimilated with the CRV Houdini's Ghost, all kept their original ranks except Rebellion officers O-4 and above. In the case of Rebellion O-4s, aka department heads, only one current department head may have Rebellion origins. The rest have Coalition origins, and were assigned their positions on the Houdini's Ghost before the incident (see our prologue).

Former Rebellion department heads either died in the incident, or downgraded to O-3 and presently serve under their Coalition counterparts as regular doctors, engineers, scientists, or lieutenants. Members may elect to make these characters or kill them off in their backstory. Both side's original first mates are deceased, leading to the current arrangement of the former Rebellion captain as the current first mate.
